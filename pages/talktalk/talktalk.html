The problem

I was hired by TalkTalk in 2014 to work on their first responsive Homepage. I have created a few early designs and soon realised I will need a design system if I want to keep consistency in design and in build across TalkTalk's online products. There are vast independent sections of TalkTalk's website such as 'Help & Support', 'Community', 'My Account', 'TV & News Portal'. All those independent sections had to be re-designed and re-built. This was a huge task that required certain level of automation in Design but also in Front-end implementation.


This required entirely new strategy and planning. The most challenging part was getting that elusive 'Buy-in' from our senior stakeholders and The Business in general to secure the funding.


I have partnered up with our Principle Developer and Architect to come up with a strategy and most efficient way of implementing a design system.


Our approach

TalkTalk is a budget Internet Service Provider and we knew we will have to rely on frameworks to keep the budget as low as possible. After extensive research and tests, we have decided to go with AngularJS and Bootstrap. Our vision was to treat TalkTalk's online products as independent Single Page Web Applications, supported by already established responsive framework re-styled with TalkTalk's brand guidelines in mind. We were asked to create a working prototype within a month and present it to the stakeholders.
